This post is tough to write because Flock to Fedora is my favorite
conference and last year's Flock might have been my favorite
conference I've ever been to. I love the Fedora community, Prague is
beautiful, the venue is nice, we always have so many interesting talks
and workshops, the organizers do an amazing job to prepare this event
for us, so I feel really guilty that I did not have much fun this
year. That is 100% on me, though.

It wasn't exactly the wisest decision ever to run my first
half-marathon the very evening before the conference and then waking
up early to commute there. I was fine physically but I couldn't pay
attention to anything because nothing felt exciting. That's the most
lifeless I've felt in a long time.

So in case anyone is wondering ... it was me, not you.


## Highlights

- Forging Fedora Project’s Future With Forgejo - Great job on the
  migration from pagure.io to forge.fedoraproject.org. It was handled
  exceptionally well. Next we are looking forward to the migration of
  src.fedoraproject.org to Forgejo, which can't happen soon
  enough. Tomáš Hrčka teased us, that it may take considerably less
  time because they already know how to do things (e.g. deploy
  forgejo, etc)
- DIY AI: Build a Private, Customizable Chatbot on Lean Hardware - Ellis Low
  showed us how to run a local LLM on our laptops and how to interact with
  it. I really appreciated him saying that except for this one small text-in,
  text-out magic blackbox, everything else is standard software engineering as
  we know it.
- Fedora Council Strategic Proposals + FESCo Q&A - Most of the discussion
  revolved around decline in new contributors and us failing to connect with the
  new generation. "They talk about changing a wallpaper, we talk about burnout"
  -- Aleksandra Fedorova
- What's Cooking in Copr, Testing Farm, tmt, Packit and Log Detective - I found
  Pavel Raiskup's return to the stage hilarious
- I ran into my former Copr team mate Adam Šamalík in the hallway and we had a
  nice chat about life. When we still worked together (10 years ago, oh how the
  time flies) I randomly asked him what did he do on a weekend. "We had no
  plans, so we bought plane tickets and went on a date to Amsterdam with my
  girlfriend". Fucking what?! That was the coolest response ever, and since that
  day, I remember it every time my creaking, lazy bones struggle to do anything
  spontanious. Why am I telling it now? Adam is now happily living in Holand
  full time. Funny how one impromptu decision can completely change your life
- Made friends with Emmanuel Seyman. He said hello after seeing the Fedora
  Podcast episode with myself as a guest. I really enjoyed our chat, and I am
  going to follow-up online because I think he'll really like Sundaram
  krishnan's project coprtree
- Miro Hrončok shared some of his idea about improving the Fedora Package Review
  process with me. This deserves an article on its own, so stay tuned.
